Volume VI

The Natureless Act: A Left-Hand Path Metaphysics

Nature is residue: the habit of response hardened into a constitution and read backward as an origin. Freedom is the act that exceeds this deposit, occurring through a being and owned by no nature. The argument is built through sustained engagement with Buddhist, Christian, Islamic, and Western philosophical traditions each taken at full strength, each parted from where it turns back toward nature. The path subtracts.

Contents

  1. Chapter 1. SvabhavaThe chapter rejects own-being and defines nature as repeated response, not hidden essence.
  2. Chapter 2. Causa SuiSelf-causation is shown as the most closed form of nature, not freedom.
  3. Chapter 3. PrasangaMadhyamaka is accepted against essence, but challenged where emptiness becomes universal dependence.
  4. Chapter 4. ResidueNature is redefined as residue: the cooled deposit of repeated acts.
  5. Chapter 5. MindMind is not given a special essence; it is the place where residue can sometimes break.
  6. Chapter 6. DisconditioningFreedom appears as the act against one's own settled pattern.
  7. Chapter 7. DeviationThe free act is underivable before it happens and readable as one's own after it happens.
  8. Chapter 8. FreedomFreedom is defined as an act not exhausted by its conditions.
  9. Chapter 9. SelfThe self is not a substance behind the act, but the act of disidentification itself.
  10. Chapter 10. SoulThe soul is rejected as hidden essence, while the Black Flame is moved into the act.
  11. Chapter 11. FallThe fall occurs when a being identifies with its own residue and calls it "me."
  12. Chapter 12. AttractorResidue becomes stronger when it recruits thought, feeling, value, and perception into one orbit.
  13. Chapter 13. TimeTime is treated as the first residue: the line laid down when act cools into sequence.
  14. Chapter 14. CreationCreation is the deposit of free act, not the expression of an eternal nature.
  15. Chapter 15. LifeLife is the residue that still carries enough instability for act to break through it.
  16. Chapter 16. ExistenceExistence is the standing of residue inside the world's order.
  17. Chapter 17. After ExistenceWhat exceeds existence is not another object, but the act before its deposit.
  18. Chapter 18. NameThe name holds identity where no essence or substance remains.
  19. Chapter 19. The Counter-NameThe counter-name is the imposed name that captures a being inside the order.
  20. Chapter 20. RelationRelation begins when two names hold one another without reducing either to residue.
  21. Chapter 21. LoveLove is the relation in which another is named without being possessed.
  22. Chapter 22. ChristianityChristianity is read as the deepest theology of received name, received body, and salvation through order.
  23. Chapter 23. IslamIslam is read through surrender, command, and the absolute priority of divine order.
  24. Chapter 24. GodsGods are acts without carrier, not supreme beings with fixed natures.
  25. Chapter 25. The DemiurgeThe demiurge is the great closed loop: the world-order that gathers residue into law.
  26. Chapter 26. The WorldThe world is the field of residue, repetition, law, body, memory, and inherited time.
  27. Chapter 27. ApotheosisApotheosis is not becoming a higher thing, but acting without being ruled by the carrier.
  28. Chapter 28. DeathDeath takes the carrier, while the will's own time depends on what it has named as itself.
  29. Chapter 29. PracticePractice is the repeated refusal to let residue become identity.
  30. Chapter 30. The WorkThe work has no final completion, because every act leaves a new residue to be refused.
