Volume VI

The Natureless Act: A Left-Hand Path Metaphysics

The Natureless Act builds a Left-Hand Path metaphysics from a single claim: the absence of fixed nature is the condition of freedom. The free act is what no prior essence, law, or system can contain, and the book follows it through selfhood, relation, world, death, and godhood. Its central move is self-naming: the refusal to let inherited sediment, given identity, or cosmic order decide what the self is. Freedom is the power of each act to break the authority of what has already been formed.

Contents

  1. Chapter 1. SvabhavaThe chapter rejects own-being and defines nature as repeated response rather than hidden essence.
  2. Chapter 2. Causa SuiSelf-causation appears as the most closed form of nature.
  3. Chapter 3. PrasangaMadhyamaka is accepted in its attack on essence and challenged where emptiness becomes universal dependence.
  4. Chapter 4. ResidueNature is redefined as residue: the cooled deposit of repeated acts.
  5. Chapter 5. MindMind has no special essence. It is the place where residue can sometimes break.
  6. Chapter 6. DisconditioningFreedom appears in the act against one's own settled pattern.
  7. Chapter 7. DeviationThe free act is underivable before it happens and readable as one's own after it happens.
  8. Chapter 8. FreedomFreedom is an act that exceeds its conditions.
  9. Chapter 9. SelfThe self is the act of disidentification from one's own residue.
  10. Chapter 10. SoulThe soul is stripped of hidden essence, and the Black Flame is moved into the act.
  11. Chapter 11. FallThe fall occurs when a being identifies with its own residue and calls it "me."
  12. Chapter 12. AttractorResidue becomes an attractor when it draws thought, feeling, value, and perception into one orbit.
  13. Chapter 13. TimeTime is treated as the first residue: the line laid down when act cools into sequence.
  14. Chapter 14. CreationCreation is the deposit of free act, not the expression of eternal nature.
  15. Chapter 15. LifeLife is residue still unstable enough for act to break through.
  16. Chapter 16. ExistenceExistence is the standing of residue inside the world's order.
  17. Chapter 17. After ExistenceWhat exceeds existence is the act before its deposit.
  18. Chapter 18. NameThe name holds identity where essence and substance have fallen away.
  19. Chapter 19. The Counter-NameThe counter-name captures a being inside an imposed identity.
  20. Chapter 20. RelationRelation begins when two names hold one another across the gap.
  21. Chapter 21. LoveLove names another without possession.
  22. Chapter 22. ChristianityChristianity is read as the deepest theology of received name, received body, and salvation through order.
  23. Chapter 23. IslamIslam is read through surrender, command, and the absolute priority of divine order.
  24. Chapter 24. GodsGods are acts without carrier, free of fixed nature.
  25. Chapter 25. The DemiurgeThe demiurge is the great closed loop: the world-order that gathers residue into law.
  26. Chapter 26. The WorldThe world is the field of residue, repetition, law, body, memory, and inherited time.
  27. Chapter 27. ApotheosisApotheosis means acting beyond the rule of the carrier.
  28. Chapter 28. DeathDeath takes the carrier, while the will's own time depends on what it has named as itself.
  29. Chapter 29. PracticePractice is the repeated refusal of identity with residue.
  30. Chapter 30. The WorkThe work has no final completion, because every act leaves new residue to be refused.
