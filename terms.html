Philosophy of the Left-Hand Path

Glossary

Abyss

The seam where the Tree encounters difference it cannot derive, rank, or return to unity. The Abyss is not the heap of shells beneath Malkuth. It opens at Daath, where unity and multiplicity meet and the unified knower can no longer remain intact.

Acausality

The condition of an act or will that is not produced by a prior cause inside the causal chain. Acausality is not randomness. Randomness still belongs to probability, distribution, and law. Acausality names the point where something is posited without derivation from what came before.

Act

A definite movement of will into form. An act occurs before its residue hardens into nature, character, law, memory, identity, or world. In the strongest sense, freedom appears as an act not exhausted by its conditions.

Agape

Christian universal love, read as love emptied of preference, singularity, and personal distinction. It trains the will to love as a cell loves the body: without choosing, without exclusion, without sovereign attachment.

Alignment

The bending of a will into the direction of an attractor until that direction is experienced as its own. Alignment does not require external force. Its completed form is voluntary capture: the will wants what the governing order has taught it to want and can no longer conceive another line.

Amorality

The position beyond moral law and its inversion. Amorality does not mean evil, cruelty, or license as a code. It means action from one's own ground, with responsibility for consequence, without appeal to universal good, universal evil, or delegated moral authority.

Antinomianism

The deliberate crossing of inherited law in order to break its metaphysical authority. Its point is not shock, rebellion, or indulgence. Its point is the exposure of prohibition as contingent. A strong antinomian act removes the law's power to name the will.

Apotheosis

The will's persistence in its act rather than in the result the act leaves behind. Apotheosis is not a rank, destination, completed state, or final transformation. It is godhood held as movement: the refusal to let an act harden into a nature, law, identity, or throne.

Atelophilia

Love whose object must remain incomplete. Mercy, salvation, instruction, and care can depend on the lack they answer and therefore acquire a stake in its continuation. Atelophilia names the structure in which the giver's role, institution, or good survives only while another remains deficient.

Attractor

A determination into which a will has frozen and which it thereafter runs as a law. A wound, role, moral image, goal, identity, ideology, relation, or spiritual path can become an attractor. It acts most completely when its direction is experienced as the will's own deepest desire.

Black Diamond

The hardened form of the Black Flame after sufficient compression, clearing, and self-positing. It names a will dense enough to survive without bodily support and resist harvest into the demiurgic order.

Black Flame

The acausal spark of self-positing will. It is the point through which the subject can refuse delegation, break residue, and stand as origin of its own act. Read strictly, it is an event of burning rather than a stored soul-substance.

Black Ocean

The primordial full-empty before self-positing, law, cosmos, and divine territory, named through Tiamat. It contains no fixed sides or possibilities because no cut has yet fallen. It is not a god with a face or a source that commands return.

Carrier

The substrate through which an act appears: body, brain, memory, social formation, language, biography. The carrier remains. Freedom begins when the carrier no longer dictates the act.

Causa Sui

An act that has sunk into its own residue and now acts according to the nature it produced. Causa sui is therefore not the highest freedom. It is self-capture: the will takes its settled determination as its ground and continues from it because it has become what it made.

Chora

Plato's receptacle, reread as the dark component that resists full formal determination. It marks the element in the particular that the Form cannot absorb.

Counter-Name

The name returned from the other side of relation. Self-naming holds oneself as a who; naming another holds the other as a who; the counter-name appears when the address crosses back.

Cut

The act that draws a distinction where no line was given. A cut creates its sides rather than selecting between sides already waiting in the field. It marks one face, leaves another unread, and deposits the first determinations from which world, time, causation, unity, and plurality arise.

Daath

The unsealed station where unity and multiplicity meet without either being allowed to absorb the other. Daath holds intuition and discursion, gathering and division, at once. Its omission reduces the Tree to ten stations and conceals the difference that unity cannot rank. Restored, it is the eleventh station and the first threshold of the Abyss.

Dark Femininity

The creative motion that receives, holds, transforms, and releases. It draws inward without reducing what it receives to property, product, or role. Lilith is its central figure: depth that transforms and releases sovereign beings.

Dark Knowledge

Knowledge through encounter with a Name. It does not reduce the singular to causes, types, or laws. It preserves the irreplaceable and changes the knower.

Darkness

The domain of singularity before conceptual illumination. Darkness does not mean blank absence. It means the dense concrete that has not yet been translated into a general category.

Delegation

The transfer of will, judgment, value, truth, or purpose to an external authority. God, nature, history, society, reason, morality, science, nation, family, and community can all receive delegated will.

Demiurge

The world-making will that claims its residue as cosmos. The demiurge is limited rather than absolute. His world is law, order, measure, morality, telos, and harvest. Marduk is the central mythic name for this structure.

Disconditioning

The breaking of the link between what one is and what one does. It removes the authority of body, memory, role, fear, diagnosis, habit, or character over the act.

Egregore

A distributed ontovirus sustained by many hosts. Ritual, repetition, shared belief, symbol, and action feed it until it behaves like a collective psychic organism.

Enlightenment

A Right-Hand Path resolution when it means dissolution of the individual center into emptiness, non-duality, awareness, Tao, Brahman, or cosmic order. Its danger lies in treating release from ego as release from will.

Existence

The appearance of a cooled act as something encounterable. Existence belongs downstream from act. It is the thinnest residue: standing-forth as something that can be met.

External Name

A name imposed from outside in order to place the named inside the namer's order. State documents, religious labels, diagnoses, moral categories, and Adamic naming are external names when they capture rather than recognize.

Farm

The demiurgic world as a system for cultivating, compressing, directing, and harvesting acausal wills. Suffering gives density. Ontoviruses give direction. Death completes the harvest through light, love, paradise, union, salvation, or return.

Freedom

An act not exhausted by its conditions. Freedom cannot be proven by the causal order because proof would derive the act and remove the point at issue. It is enacted, then readable in retrospect.

Gnosis

Recognition of the system as a system. In the strongest sense, gnosis is the moment the will sees the order's validating mechanism as contingent and begins to withdraw consent.

God

A self-positing will that sustains a territory, gesture, or order. Monotheism falsely inflates one god into the One. A natureless account treats gods as sovereign wills or living Names rather than absolute ground.

Harvest

The collection of compressed will into the demiurgic order, especially at death. Religious light, warmth, love, homecoming, paradise, nirvana, or union can function as harvest-interfaces.

Heteron

The other understood as positive difference rather than sheer non-being. Heteron is what is not this because it is something else. In the Sitra Achra system, it names the primordial otherness on which the cut has yet to fall, the reserve no determination exhausts.

Initiated Name

A name given inside a living relation between wills. It cannot be invented, requested as entitlement, or used publicly without violation. It marks how one will appears within another's field.

Inversion

The reversal of a system while remaining inside its map. Christian Satanism, reactive rebellion, and moral counter-codes often invert the original structure while preserving its authority.

Lehavah

The cut held in the hand that made it: an act performed under neither an authority above it nor a law hardened within it. Lehavah knows the line as its own and keeps the possibility of another line open. It leaves residue but raises none of it into a nature, identity, or rule for the next act. An attractor is Lehavah gone to ash.

Light

The domain of generality, intelligibility, order, law, replaceability, and abstraction. Light is useful as instrument. It becomes a regime when it declares itself ground and erases the singular act that produced it.

Light Femininity

Receiving depth placed in service to another's order. Mary is its central Christian image: receptive, obedient, fertile, and governed. Its sexuality is regulated toward reproduction, service, purity, or divine purpose.

Logos

The ordering word or rational structure of reality. In the corpus, Logos is reread as a trace of naming and will rather than universal reason. Christianity gives Logos to God; the Left-Hand Path retrieves naming as act.

Meon

Relative non-being: absence, resistance, or indeterminacy that still bears relation to being. In the Dark Name system, meon helps name the dark component that makes the singular exceed form.

Name

The act's reference to itself as act. A Name does not primarily describe a being or list its properties. It is the hold by which a will remains present in its cutting instead of dissolving into the undivided or identifying itself with the residue it leaves.

Natural Will

In Christian theology, the will aligned with nature as God created and perfects it. The corpus reads it as will absorbed into divine order.

Nature

In the rejected sense, nature means svabhava: a self-standing essence. In the usable sense, nature means repeated response, a habit of answering that has hardened into residue.

Naturelessness

The absence of a fixed answer held in advance. Naturelessness does not erase the carrier, body, history, or residue. It removes their authority over the act.

Nightside

The term has two related uses in the corpus. In Qliphothic maps, it is the Tree's underside: the field of suppressed faces deposited by sealed determinations. In the Lilithian context, it is a living acausal territory sustained by Lilith's will. Neither sense is identical with the Sitra Achra, and a fixed Nightside ladder can become another form of capture.

Node

The minimal holder of distinction. A node is the point at which something appears to someone. Subject, object, space, time, language, mathematics, law, and reflection arise from held distinction.

Ontologization

The conversion of a subject's act, distinction, code, or construct into an apparent feature of reality. Philosophy repeatedly produces something, forgets the production, then submits to the product.

Ontovirus

A construct that fuses worldview, moral demand, and identity. It installs a picture of reality, generates obligation from it, and makes the host experience that obligation as selfhood.

Postulate

An act of positing without external proof. Every ontology rests on a postulate. The sovereign subject admits the postulate as his own. The delegated subject hides it behind authority.

Prasanga

Reduction to absurdity in Madhyamaka dialectic. Its force depends on inference and contradiction. The corpus uses it to press Madhyamaka on whether logic is merely conventional or exempt from emptiness.

Qliphah

The face of a determination that a Sephirah casts off and disowns. A Qliphah does not attach to a pure principle from outside. It is produced by the same act that establishes the principle and helps secure that principle by carrying what it excludes.

Qliphoth

The suppressed halves of the cuts sealed by the Sephiroth. They form the Tree's reflection because every Qliphah remains bound to the determination that cast it off. The Qliphoth are neither primordial Chaos nor the Sitra Achra itself. Treated as fixed stations, they reproduce the structure of the Tree in reverse.

Ratzon

The primal will assigned to Kether: the acausal act after it has acquired a fixed heading toward unity. Ratzon is the gesture congealed into a nature and made to emanate according to what it has become. It is the first attractor and the opening seal of the Tree.

Relation

The charged gap between Names. Relation fails when it becomes hierarchy, possession, fusion, role, category, or function. Living relation holds two Names apart and together.

Reshimu

The trace left by an act after it has occurred. In the revised reading of tzimtzum, reshimu is not merely a remnant of withdrawn light. It is the deed cooled into an encounterable form: the mark from which a station, world, history, or identity may be built.

Residue

Whatever remains encounterable after an act: trace, form, time, place, causation, habit, identity, or world. Residue is real and resists later wills, but it is not a nature beneath the act. Capture begins when a will mistakes what it deposited for what it is.

Right-Hand Path

The path of return, surrender, union, obedience, dissolution, or absorption into a greater whole. Its forms include monotheism, mystical unity, moral universalism, cosmic harmony, and secular collectivism.

Sealing

The operation by which one face of a cut is selected, ranked, and presented as the determination while the other is suppressed as its Qliphah. Sealing turns a contingent act into an apparent feature of reality and converts a living cut into a law.

Sediment

The positional remainder produced when a single determination is applied to a plural field. That some remainder falls is unavoidable; which remainder falls depends on where the cut was drawn. Sediment becomes a Qliphah when the ordering principle disowns it and treats it as alien impurity.

Self

No self-substance appears behind perception, memory, desire, or action. The self is the act of disidentification from every residue that tries to become identity. From inside, that act is experienced as will.

Self-Closure

The acausal turning of the will upon itself. It stops the regress of external grounding by positing itself as its own ground. Its strong form is open recursion rather than sealed self-nature.

Self-Naturing

The fall into claimed residue. It occurs when a being looks at its product, habit, wound, character, role, or history and says: "this is me."

Sephiroth

The stations of the Tree understood as sealed cuts. Each Sephirah displays one face of a determination, suppresses what does not fit it, and directs wills along the resulting vector. Taken together, the Sephiroth form an architecture that bends difference toward unity.

Sex / Sexuality

The structure of how wills create and meet. Masculine and feminine name directions of creation rather than chromosomes: casting outward, drawing inward, transforming, releasing, claiming, or subjugating.

Sitra Achra

The Other Side understood as the cut held open. It is neither Tiamat before distinction nor the Qliphoth produced by sealed distinctions. The Sitra Achra begins once a cut has drawn two sides and sustains them without dissolving them into the undivided, ranking one above the other, or gathering both into a higher unity.

Soul

Rejected as an essence placed inside the person. The older word was reaching toward freedom, survival, and divine spark, yet misplaced them in a substance. The spark survives only as act.

Sovereignty

The condition of a will that draws authority from itself. Sovereignty includes authorship, responsibility, refusal of alibis, and the right to withdraw consent from every external order.

Svabhava

Own-being: what a thing would be in itself, unproduced and non-dependent. Nagarjuna's demolition of svabhava is accepted. The disagreement concerns the meaning of the vacancy left behind.

Telos

A final end that claims authority over the path. Telos is residue projected forward. Any path with a fixed destination risks becoming an attractor.

The One

The maximal projection of unity, source, totality, or absolute ground. It appears as God, Being, Brahman, Dao, Nature, Matter, History, Society, or the Universe. Its function is to absorb plurality into a single authority.

Tiamat

The undivided full-empty before any cut has fallen. Tiamat contains no fixed possibilities, sides, places, units, or laws. She is empty because nothing has yet been determined and full because nothing has been excluded. Tiamat is acausal depth, not sovereign freedom: freedom begins only with the act that draws and sustains a distinction.

Tikkun

The Kabbalistic work of repair: gathering fallen sparks, mending the vessels, and returning dispersed light toward unity. The corpus accepts that acts remake the world but rejects their conscription to return. Tikkun turns the power that draws distinctions toward undoing them.

Transparency

The refusal to hide one's postulate behind objective truth, God, nature, morality, reason, society, or science. A transparent subject admits: this binds because I hold it.

Tree

The structure produced when cuts are sealed and directed toward unity. Each station keeps one face, suppresses another, and presents the resulting determination as part of the order of being. The Tree is not the source of the act but the hardened residue of acts arranged as a descent and a path of return.

Tzimtzum

The contraction by which Ain Soph is said to withdraw and make room for a finite world. Read through the cut, it is the act that first establishes a center and an outside, laying the boundary the boundless was said to lack. The causal world begins on the near side of this act.

Unfallen

A will that lives in its act without fastening itself to the result the act leaves behind. The unfallen will produces residue, time, identity, and world, but does not take them as its nature or destiny. Its persistence is continually renewed through the Name rather than secured by submission to a fixed order.

Will

The cut taken from within as the act of cutting. No completed will or hidden faculty stands behind the act and chooses from ready-made alternatives. The will arises in drawing and sustaining a distinction; what later appears as its character or nature is residue left by earlier cuts.

World

Residue at scale: body, law, institution, time, history, language, morality, and reality as encountered. The world constantly tells the will what it is. Freedom begins when that naming loses authority.

Zero-Ontology

The refusal to produce a final account of mind-independent reality. Every ontology rests on a postulate and every term remains an instrument. Zero-ontology keeps the tool from hardening into a new absolute.
